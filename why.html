WHY SERIOUS?

Under the influence of traditional Confucian culture, man is presented first as a member of the family rather than as a person in his own right. That is, in China, privacy is more collective. This is essentially different from the starting point of privacy in the West. Moreover, the state is considered to be an extension of the home, so we are not so resistant to "surrendering privacy to managers". This is my reflection as a Chinese, and I hope that in some way I can tell you. There is no right or wrong culture, and there is no right or wrong culture of privacy, but people should know what kind of environment they are in, and what kind of mode they are thinking in.
